My encounter with tech

My encounter with Technology (computer programming) is rather epic. It all started during my senior class in highschool.
Dad signed me as an apprentice with a computer technician. I only go to him during holidays since I was always busy during school sessions.

What we did then were to format and reinstall windows systems, install drivers and install packages. We also troubleshoot motherboard and replace components that are easily replaceable. We don't replace chips though. We rather tell the customers to buy a new motherboard when a chip has been damaged.

On a particular day while trying to make backup a customer's computer, my boss external HDD got infected by virus. Each time he tried to open the hardrive, the virus would corrupt more files.
I carefully watched my boss as he opened a black console and typed some mysterious words. In awe I watched as file names and paths scrolled up the screen.
What is that? I asked my boss. CMD he mumble? How does it work boss? I asked again. My boss never answered me. Maybe he would give me an answer when I see him tomorrow I don't know.

Determined to understand what my boss did, I went home, sat at the computer and ran CMD. I stared at the black console with no idea at all. Then I started to type some random stuff. Just when I was getting tired, I typed help. And for the first time, something meaningful came out. That night, I did my "file copy" operation on cmd before I went to bed. And that is how it began. I would then borrow my dad's phone and search for more commands and usage until I came accross something called batch scripting.

MY FIRST BATCH SCRIPT LOOKS LIKE THIS:

top:
        echo "hello world"
        pause
goto top
